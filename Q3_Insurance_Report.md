# Q3 2025 Financial and Operational Performance Report
**Company:** Vanguard Mutual Insurance Group  
**Prepared By:** Office of the Chief Financial Officer & Actuarial Strategy Team  
**Date:** October 15, 2025  
**Confidentiality:** INTERNAL USE ONLY - TIER 1 STRICT  

## 1. Executive Summary
Vanguard Mutual Insurance Group delivered a resilient, albeit challenged, financial performance in the third quarter of 2025. The organization navigated a highly complex macroeconomic environment characterized by stubborn core inflation, supply chain bottlenecks in the auto-repair sector, and an unprecedented frequency of severe convective storms in the Midwest. Gross Written Premiums (GWP) grew by 8.4% year-over-year to $4.25 billion, a figure largely driven by aggressive, targeted rate increases in our Commercial Auto and Property lines, alongside steady retention in the Personal Lines segment. 

Despite this strong top-line premium growth, the overall Combined Ratio deteriorated to 101.2%, up from 99.8% in Q3 2024. This pushes us slightly into underwriting unprofitability for the quarter. The primary drivers of this deterioration were a $120 million gross loss stemming from Hurricane Helene and continued, severe social inflation impacting bodily injury litigation payouts. 

Net Income for the quarter stood at $145 million. This was heavily supported by stronger-than-expected net investment income, which offset the underwriting losses. Management remains hyper-focused on three core pillars for the remainder of the year: securing rate adequacy in highly regulated states, accelerating the deployment of our AI-driven claims triage system, and executing strict expense management protocols across all non-essential vendor contracts.

### 2.2 Investment Portfolio Performance
Our investment portfolio proved to be the anchor for Q3 profitability, generating $310 million in net investment income—a robust 16.9% increase year-over-year. The Treasury team's decision to strategically shorten the portfolio duration in Q1 to mitigate interest rate volatility paid massive dividends this quarter. 

The new money yield reached 5.4%, significantly outpacing the roll-off yield of older bonds maturing at 3.8%. Our asset allocation remains highly conservative to protect policyholder surplus. The current mix sits at 85% in investment-grade fixed maturities (primarily municipal and corporate bonds), 10% in public equities (focused on dividend-yielding blue chips), and 5% in alternative private credit vehicles.

## 3. Segment Performance Analysis

### 3.1 Personal Lines (Auto and Homeowners)
Personal lines remain Vanguard's largest segment by premium volume, but they faced the heaviest margin pressure this quarter.

**Personal Auto:** The Auto loss ratio climbed sharply to 78.1%. While the pandemic-era supply chain issues for replacement parts (like microchips and steel) have largely normalized, the severity of bodily injury claims continues to rise. We are seeing aggressive plaintiff litigation tactics and higher medical billing costs driving up average severity by 11% compared to last year. In response, we implemented a blended 6.5% rate increase across 14 states, which will begin earning into our NEP in Q4.

**Homeowners:** This segment experienced significant volatility. Excluding catastrophic weather events, the underlying attritional loss ratio actually improved by 1.2 points. This underlying improvement reflects the success of stricter underwriting guidelines introduced last year, specifically regarding mandatory roof-age inspections and the implementation of percentage-based wind/hail deductibles. However, catastrophe losses ultimately dragged the segment into unprofitability for the quarter.

### 3.2 Commercial Lines
Commercial lines outperformed internal forecasts, acting as a crucial stabilizing force.

**Workers' Compensation:** This remains our most profitable line of business, boasting an exceptional combined ratio of 88.4%. Claim frequency trends continue to decline, driven by investments in workplace safety technologies (such as wearable ergonomic sensors) and a permanent shift toward remote work in the clerical and professional sectors we insure.

**Commercial Property:** GWP in this segment grew by an impressive 12%. This was due to robust rate momentum in the broader market, as competitors restricted capacity. We successfully reduced our exposure to high-risk coastal tier-1 wind zones by 8%, intentionally shedding unprofitable accounts while relying more heavily on our newly negotiated reinsurance treaties to cap our downside risk on the remaining portfolio.

## 4. Claims, Weather Events, and Actuarial Adjustments

### 4.1 Catastrophe (CAT) Losses
The third quarter is historically Vanguard's most volatile period for weather-related claims, and 2025 was no exception. Total CAT losses amounted to $245 million net of reinsurance recoveries, adding 6.4 points to the overall loss ratio. This exceeded our internal Q3 catastrophe budget by $40 million. 

The primary driver was Hurricane Helene, which made landfall as a Category 3 storm in the Southeast, causing widespread wind and flood damage across our Florida and Georgia books of business. Additionally, two unseasonal derecho events in the Midwest resulted in 14,000 localized hail claims in a single 48-hour period in August.

### 4.2 Actuarial Reserve Development
Following a comprehensive actuarial review completed in late August, the company was required to recognize $35 million in adverse prior-year reserve development. 

This adjustment was entirely isolated to the 2022 and 2023 accident years within the Commercial Auto liability line. Our actuaries determined that the initial reserves set during those years were insufficient to cover the "long-tail" litigation settlements currently materializing in the court system. We have consequently adjusted the ultimate loss selections upward for those specific cohorts. Current accident year (2025) reserves are deemed fully adequate and carry a 5% margin for error above the actuarial midpoint.

## 5. Regulatory Environment and State-Level Dynamics

Navigating the fragmented state regulatory landscape remains a significant headwind to achieving target margins, particularly in our two largest markets: California and New York.

**California:** The Department of Insurance has drastically slowed the approval process for Auto rate filings. We currently have a 12.5% rate increase pending approval, which has been stalled in the intervention process for over eight months. Consequently, we have paused all new business advertising in the state and tightened underwriting rules to restrict growth until rate adequacy is achieved.

**New York:** Regulatory scrutiny around telematics and usage-based insurance (UBI) pricing models has intensified. We are currently undergoing a market conduct exam regarding our "Safe-Drive" mobile application data usage. We anticipate resolving this with the state regulators by November without material fines, but it has delayed the rollout of our V2 pricing algorithm in the Northeast region.

## 6. Strategic Initiatives and Technology Integration

### 6.1 Project "Clear-Path" (AI Claims Automation)
In September, the IT and Claims departments successfully deployed Phase 1 of our AI-driven "Clear-Path" initiative. Utilizing large language models and computer vision pipelines, the system now automatically processes, estimates, and settles Tier-3 (low complexity) claims—specifically auto glass replacement and minor single-vehicle collision claims—without human adjuster intervention. 

Early telemetry metrics are highly encouraging. The system has reduced the average end-to-end processing time for eligible claims from 4.5 days down to 12 minutes. This automation saved an estimated $2.2 million in loss adjustment expenses (LAE) this quarter alone, and we plan to expand the system's capabilities to handle low-severity water damage claims in the Homeowners line by Q1 2026.

### 6.2 Data Warehouse Modernization
We successfully completed the migration of our legacy on-premise actuarial databases to a modern, cloud-native Snowflake environment. This transition reduces data query latency by 40% and allows our pricing teams to run complex stochastic weather simulations daily, rather than weekly, significantly improving our real-time risk visibility.

## 7. Forward Outlook and Q4 Guidance
Looking ahead to the fourth quarter, macroeconomic indicators suggest that core inflation is finally cooling, which should begin to stabilize physical damage severities and repair costs. However, we remain highly cautious regarding the unpredictable nature of early winter weather events in the Northeast and Midwest.

Our primary operational focus will be pushing our rate filings across the finish line in California and deploying the next phase of our automated claims triage. We project Q4 GWP growth to moderate slightly to between 6% and 8%. Assuming a normalized fourth-quarter weather pattern, we aim to pull the overall combined ratio back beneath the 100% profitability threshold by year-end, targeting a 99.0% finish for the full fiscal year 2025.
